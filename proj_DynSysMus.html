Overview

When working on my final for Dr. Nia Amazeen's dynamics of behavior class I was also deeply engrossed in my work in Dr. Mina Johnson's Embodied Games lab which sought to explore the connection between embodiment, digital media, and pedagogy. It seemed to me that a reasonable thing to do would be to combine the two, so I designed a 'museum' in which users get to explore a series of interactive exhibitions concerning dynamical systems, with the idea being that embodiment of control parameter manipulation and immediate visual feedback might help students to understand the feedback loops that govern the behavior of dynamical systems.
